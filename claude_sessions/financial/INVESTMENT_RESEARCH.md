## Part 1: Traditional Investment Strategies

### Momentum Investing

**What It Is:** Buy assets that have performed well recently, expecting continued performance.

**Recommended Implementation:**
- **12-1 Month Momentum**: Rank stocks by 12-month return, excluding most recent month
- Quarterly or annual rebalancing (monthly is for professionals)
- Portfolio of 50-100 stocks for best results

**Key ETFs:**
| ETF | Name | Focus |
|-----|------|-------|
| MTUM | iShares MSCI USA Momentum | Large-cap momentum |
| QMOM | Alpha Architect US Quantitative Momentum | Pure momentum factor |

**Performance Context:** Momentum has historically outperformed S&P 500 over long periods. Works best in trending markets.

**Source:** [Interactive Brokers Campus](https://www.interactivebrokers.com/campus/traders-insight/securities/technical-analysis/momentum-investing-a-simple-strategy-that-has-historically-outperformed-the-sp-500/)

---

### Value + Momentum Combination

**Why Combine:** They perform well at different times. Value does well in recoveries; momentum does well in trends.

**Implementation:**
- 50% Value ETF (VTV, VLUE)
- 50% Momentum ETF (MTUM, QMOM)
- Annual rebalancing

**Benefit:** Similar returns to pure momentum with fewer painful drawdowns.

**Source:** [Quant Investing](https://www.quant-investing.com/blog/should-you-mix-value-and-momentum-strategies)

---

### Mean Reversion Trading

**What It Is:** Bet that prices will return to their average after extreme moves.

**Best Indicators:**
- Bollinger Bands (buy below lower band, sell above upper)
- RSI below 30 = oversold (buy signal)
- RSI above 70 = overbought (sell signal)

**Best Markets:** Range-bound, volatile periods. NOT strong trends.

**Hold Period:** 3-7 days typically

**Risk Warning:** Strong trends can exceed "extreme" levels for extended periods. Use stop losses.

**Source:** [TrendSpider](https://trendspider.com/learning-center/mean-reversion-trading-strategies/)

## Part 4: Alternative Strategies

### I-Bonds (Series I Savings Bonds)

**Current Rate:** 4.03% (Nov 2025 - Apr 2026)
- Fixed Rate: 0.90% (permanent for life of bond)
- Variable Rate: 3.12% (adjusts with inflation)

**Limits:** $10,000 per person per calendar year

**Rules:**
- Must hold 12 months minimum
- Redeem before 5 years = forfeit 3 months interest
- Backed by US Government

**Tax Benefits:**
- Exempt from state/local taxes
- Can be tax-free if used for education

**Best Use Case:** "Tier 2" emergency fund or education savings

**Source:** [TreasuryDirect](https://www.treasurydirect.gov/savings-bonds/i-bonds/i-bonds-interest-rates/)

### Tax-Loss Harvesting

**What It Is:** Sell losing positions to offset capital gains elsewhere.

**Potential Benefit:** Add 1-2% to after-tax returns annually

**Best Practices:**
1. Don't wait until December - harvest year-round
2. Avoid wash-sale rule (30-day wait or buy similar, not identical ETF)
3. Only works in taxable accounts (not IRAs/401k)
4. Use automation (Wealthfront, Betterment) for best results

**ETF Swap Example:**
- Sell VTI at loss
- Immediately buy ITOT (similar but not "substantially identical")
- Maintain market exposure while capturing tax loss

**Source:** [Vanguard](https://investor.vanguard.com/investor-resources-education/taxes/offset-gains-loss-harvesting), [Wealthfront](https://www.wealthfront.com/tax-loss-harvesting)

---

### Weather Derivatives

**What They Are:** Financial contracts that pay based on weather conditions (not crop damage).

**How They Work:**
- Set threshold (e.g., rainfall below 2 inches)
- If threshold met, automatic payout
- No need to prove damage

**Advantages Over Crop Insurance:**
- Faster payout
- No loss adjustment
- Can cover revenue loss from bad weather

**Challenges for Small Farms:**
- "Basis risk" - weather at measuring station may differ from your farm
- Complex pricing
- Currently more accessible for larger operations

**Future Potential:** Government could use weather derivatives to reduce crop insurance program costs.

**Source:** [Space Coast Daily](https://spacecoastdaily.com/2025/06/weather-derivatives-and-agricultural-commodities-tools-for-risk-management-in-farming-economies/)

### Seasonal Cash Flow Management

**Emergency Fund Sizing:**
- Minimum: 3-6 months operating expenses
- Recommended: 20-30% of annual expenses
- Reserve during harvest: 15-20% of revenue

**Income Smoothing Strategies:**
1. **Schedule J**: Average farm income over 3 years for tax purposes
2. **Operating loans**: Time repayment to post-harvest
3. **Negotiate supplier terms**: Longer payment periods before planting
4. **Diversify income**: Off-season activities, equipment rental

**Cash Flow Budget Must Include:**
- All income sources (crops, livestock, CSA, government payments)
- All expenses (seeds, labor, equipment, loan payments)
- Seasonal timing of both

**Source:** [Iowa State Extension](https://www.extension.iastate.edu/agdm/wholefarm/html/c3-58.html), [Alerus Financial](https://www.alerus.com/financial-advice/10-strategies-for-effective-cash-flow-management-in-farming-operations/)
