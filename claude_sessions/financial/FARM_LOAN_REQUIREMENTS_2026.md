# Farm Loan Requirements Research - 2026
## Comprehensive Guide for Tiny Seed Farm OS

**Research Date:** January 24, 2026
**Purpose:** Document all requirements for farm loan programs to build unified loan application dashboard
**Target User:** Small farm operations, beginning farmers in Pennsylvania

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This document consolidates requirements for four major farm lending programs available to small and beginning farmers:

1. **Horizon Farm Credit** - Private agricultural lender with flexible programs
2. **USDA FSA Loans** - Federal direct and guaranteed loans (multiple types)
3. **PA Next Generation Farmer Loan** - State-level financing for land/equipment
4. **Beginning Farmer Programs** - Federal and state programs with tax benefits

**Key Finding:** There is significant overlap in documentation requirements across all programs, making a unified dashboard highly valuable for farmers preparing multiple applications simultaneously.

## 2. USDA FSA LOANS

**Website:** [farmers.gov](https://www.farmers.gov/loans)
**Local Office:** Find at [farmers.gov/service-locator](https://www.farmers.gov/service-locator)

The USDA Farm Service Agency offers multiple loan types, each with specific requirements:

### 2A. FARM OPERATING LOANS (Direct)

**Loan Amount:** Up to $400,000 maximum

#### Purpose
Finance costs of operating a farm or ranch including:
- Annual operating expenses
- Family living expenses
- Equipment and livestock purchases
- Minor real estate improvements

#### Eligibility Requirements

**Credit History:**
- No previous FSA debt forgiveness
- No delinquency on Federal debt (except IRS tax debt)
- Unable to obtain sufficient credit elsewhere
- Acceptable repayment history with other creditors
- **Note:** FSA does NOT use credit scores for eligibility

**Managerial Ability:**
Must demonstrate through ANY of these:
- Successful completion of farm management curriculum (Cooperative Extension, community college, vocational ag program, or Land Grant university)
- Owned/managed/operated farm business for 1+ full production/marketing cycle within 5 years
- Employment as migrant farm worker elevated to leadership
- Raised on farm with significant management responsibilities
- Successfully obtained and repaid at least 1 FSA Youth Loan

**Management complexity required varies based on:**
- Complexity of operation
- Loan amount requested

#### Required Application Forms

**Core Forms:**
- **FSA-2001:** Request for Direct Loan Assistance
- **FSA-2002:** Three-Year Financial History
- **FSA-2003:** Three-Year Production History
- **FSA-2004:** Authorization to Release Information
- **FSA-2005:** Creditor List
- **FSA-2037:** Farm Business Plan and Worksheet Balance Sheet
- **FSA-2038:** Farm Business Plan Worksheet Projected Actual Income and Expense
- **FSA-2302:** Description of Farm Training and Experience

**Additional Documents:**
- Proof of identity and citizenship (passport, driver's license, or birth certificate)
- For multiple loan types: Can combine farm ownership and operating loan on single application

#### Application Process

1. Contact local FSA office to schedule appointment with Farm Loan Officer/Manager
2. Complete application package with assistance
3. Submit all required forms and documentation
4. FSA reviews eligibility and financial capacity
5. Loan decision
6. Closing

**Sources:**
- [Farm Operating Loans | Farm Service Agency](https://www.fsa.usda.gov/resources/farm-loan-programs/farm-operating-loans)
- [FSA Farm Loan Application Quick Guide](https://www.farmers.gov/modules/custom/farmers_farm_loans/pdfs/farm-loan-application-quick-guide-individual.pdf)

### 2B. FARM OWNERSHIP LOANS (Direct)

**Standard Loan Amount:** Up to $600,000 maximum
**Down Payment Loan:** Up to $300,150 maximum

#### Purpose
- Purchase or enlarge a farm or ranch
- Construct or improve farm buildings
- Develop farm or conservation practices
- Refinance farm debt
- Pay closing costs

#### Eligibility Requirements

**Same as Operating Loans, PLUS:**
- For Down Payment Loans specifically:
  - **ONLY** available to beginning farmers/ranchers AND/OR minority and women applicants
  - Minimum 5% down payment required
  - FSA finances up to 45% of purchase price
  - Total financing from FSA + other creditors cannot exceed 95%

#### Required Documents

Same forms as Operating Loans (FSA-2001 through FSA-2302), PLUS:
- Signed purchase option, contract to buy, or similar form
- Copies of written leases (if leasing land or equipment)
- Credit reports and documentation

#### Terms
- Up to 40 years for farm real estate
- Competitive interest rates (updated monthly)

**Sources:**
- [Farm Ownership Loans | Farm Service Agency](https://www.fsa.usda.gov/resources/farm-loan-programs/farm-ownership-loans)
- [Beginning Farmers and Ranchers Loans](https://www.fsa.usda.gov/resources/beginning-farmers-and-ranchers-loans)

### 2C. MICROLOANS (Operating & Ownership)

**Maximum Loan Amount:** $50,000 for either type

#### Purpose
Specifically designed for:
- Beginning farmers
- Small family farm operators
- Non-traditional/niche farming operations

#### Unique Features

**Simplified Application:**
- Streamlined paperwork compared to standard FSA loans
- Less stringent requirements
- Faster processing

**Reduced Managerial Experience Requirements:**
- Standard: 3 years farm management experience within 10 years
- Alternative qualifications (only need ONE):
  - 16 credit hours post-secondary education in agriculture-related field
  - 1 year business management experience
  - Small business experience + any farm experience + self-guided apprenticeship

**Flexible Uses:**
- Operating Microloan: Annual expenses, equipment, supplies
- Ownership Microloan: Land purchase, construction, improvements

#### Eligibility Requirements

**Basic:**
- U.S. citizen, non-citizen national, or qualified alien
- Satisfactory credit history
- Farm management experience or education
- Unable to obtain sufficient credit elsewhere
- Not delinquent on Federal debt (except IRS)
- Not disqualified due to Federal Crop Insurance violation

**For Beginning Farmers:**
- Operated farm/ranch for less than 10 years
- Meets loan eligibility for specific program
- Substantially participates in operation
- (For ownership) Does not own farm > 30% of county median size

#### Required Documents

Same core FSA forms as other loan types, but:
- Less detailed production history accepted
- Business plan can be less comprehensive
- Application package tailored to smaller scale

#### Application Process

1. Complete and submit loan application to local FSA office
2. Use FSA Loan Assistance Tool online at farmers.gov (optional)
3. Work with FSA loan officer on simplified package
4. Approval decision
5. Closing

**Sources:**
- [Microloan Programs | Farm Service Agency](https://www.fsa.usda.gov/resources/farm-loan-programs/microloans)
- [Operating Microloan | Farm Service Agency](https://www.fsa.usda.gov/resources/programs/operating-microloan)
- [Microloans Application Quick Guide](https://www.farmers.gov/sites/default/files/documents/farmersgov-loan-quick-guide-micro.pdf)

### 2D. GUARANTEED FARM LOANS

#### Overview
FSA guarantees loans made by commercial lenders, reducing lender risk and enabling farmers to access credit they otherwise couldn't obtain.

#### How It Works
- Farmer applies through approved commercial lender
- Lender underwrites and services loan
- FSA provides guarantee (typically 90-95%)
- If borrower defaults, FSA pays lender per guarantee

#### Eligibility
Similar to direct loans but:
- Must be unable to obtain credit elsewhere without guarantee
- Commercial lender must be FSA-approved

**Source:**
- [Guaranteed Farm Loans | Farm Service Agency](https://www.fsa.usda.gov/resources/programs/guaranteed-farm-loans)

### 2E. FSA DEBT CONSOLIDATION TOOL

**Access:** [farmers.gov/debt-consolidation-tool](https://www.farmers.gov/debt-consolidation-tool)
**Launch Date:** August 2024

#### What It Is
Free online calculator that helps farmers:
- Evaluate potential savings from consolidating operating debts
- Compare FSA consolidation loans vs. local lenders
- Identify opportunities for better terms and rates

#### How to Use

1. Visit farmers.gov/debt-consolidation-tool (no login required)
2. Enter current farm operating debts:
   - Multiple loans
   - Lines of credit
   - Credit cards
3. Tool calculates potential savings from consolidation:
   - Lower interest rates
   - Reduced monthly payments
   - Total interest savings over loan term
4. Compare FSA options vs. commercial lender options
5. Use results to make informed decision

#### Benefits

**Financial:**
- Potential lower interest rates
- Single payment instead of multiple
- Extended terms can reduce payment burden
- Savings can be reinvested in operation, retirement, or education

**Strategic:**
- Better cash flow management
- Simplified debt tracking
- Improved financial planning

#### When to Consider

- Multiple high-interest operating loans
- Cash flow challenges from multiple payments
- Interest rates higher than current FSA rates
- Desire to simplify financial management

**Eligibility for FSA Debt Consolidation Loan:**
- Must meet FSA loan eligibility requirements
- Debts must be farm operating expenses
- Cannot consolidate debts with unreasonable terms

**Sources:**
- [USDA Launches Online Debt Consolidation Tool](https://www.fsa.usda.gov/news-events/news/09-04-2024/usda-launches-online-debt-consolidation-tool-increase-farmer-rancher)

## 3. PENNSYLVANIA NEXT GENERATION FARMER LOAN PROGRAM

**Administering Agency:** PA Department of Community & Economic Development (DCED)
**Website:** [dced.pa.gov/programs/next-generation-farmer-loan-program](https://dced.pa.gov/programs/next-generation-farmer-loan-program/)
**Information:** [pa.gov/aginnovation](https://www.pa.gov/aginnovation)

### Program Overview

Tax-exempt loan program providing affordable capital to next generation farmers for purchasing:
- Farmland
- Farm equipment (new and used)
- Farm buildings
- Breeding livestock

**Key Benefit:** Lower interest rates through tax-exempt bonds (typically 80-85% of bank's standard agricultural rate)

### Loan Amounts & Terms

**Maximum Loan Amount:**
- **Current (2026):** $667,000 maximum
- Up to $62,500 can be used for used equipment purchase
- **Note:** Amounts are subject to change; verify with DCED

**Loan Terms:**
- **Real Estate:** 15-25 years typically
- **Equipment/Machinery:** 5-7 years typically
- **Interest Rate:** Usually 80-85% of lender's standard ag rate

**Restrictions:**
- **Cannot** use for residence purchase
- Must be used for agricultural assets

### Eligibility Requirements

#### Beginning Farmer Qualifications

**Net Worth Limits (2026):**
- **Partnership:** Less than $1,455,446
- **Note:** Limits adjusted annually based on PA farm real estate values per USDA NASS
- Check current limits at [www.PAgrows.com/loans.aspx](http://www.PAgrows.com/loans.aspx)

**Land Ownership:**
- No prior ownership interest in substantial farmland
- Cannot exceed 30% of median farm size in county where land is located

**Residency:**
- Must be legal resident of Pennsylvania

**Farming Intent:**
- Must intend to farm in Pennsylvania
- Provide majority of labor and management

**Experience:**
- Less than 10 years receiving income from farming
- Must demonstrate experience or transferable skills in agriculture

**Other:**
- Must be at least 18 years of age

### Required Documents

**Personal:**
- Proof of Pennsylvania residency
- Identification documents
- Personal financial statement
- Tax returns (typically 3 years)

**Farm Business:**
- Farm business plan
- Projected income and expenses
- Experience documentation
- Proof of agricultural training/education (if used for qualifications)

**Property/Asset:**
- Property appraisal (for land purchase)
- Equipment quotes/invoices
- Title work
- Purchase agreements

**Financial:**
- Credit history authorization
- Current balance sheet
- Cash flow projections
- Existing debt documentation

### Application Process

1. **Verify Eligibility:** Confirm beginning farmer status and net worth limits
2. **Online Application:** Submit through Single Application for Assistance system at pa.gov
3. **Application Review:** DCED and lending institution review
4. **Lender Approval:** Commercial lender underwrites loan
5. **DCED Approval:** State approves tax-exempt status
6. **Closing:** Complete loan through approved lender

### Approved Lenders

Applications must be submitted through approved participating lenders. Contact:
- **EDC Finance Corporation:** Primary administrator - [edcfinancecorp.com](https://edcfinancecorp.com/agricultural-lending/next-generation-farmer-loan-ngflp/)
- Other PA-approved agricultural lenders

### Additional Benefits

**Tax Advantages:**
- Borrower receives lower interest rate due to tax-exempt bond structure
- Lender receives tax-exempt interest income
- Creates win-win for beginning farmers and lenders

**Realty Transfer Tax Exemption:**
- Transfer of preserved farmland to qualified beginning farmer may be exempt from realty transfer tax

**Sources:**
- [Next Generation Farmer Loan Program | PA.gov](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/pda/grants/state-programs/next-gen-farmer-loan-program)
- [Next Generation Farmer Loan Program Guidelines](https://www.pa.gov/content/dam/copapwp-pagov/en/pda/documents/funding/documents/nextgenerationfarmer_guidelines.pdf)
- [EDC Finance Corporation Next Gen Farmer Loan](https://edcfinancecorp.com/agricultural-lending/next-generation-farmer-loan-ngflp/)

### 4D. USDA Conservation Programs for Beginning Farmers

**Environmental Quality Incentives Program (EQIP):**
- **Standard Cost Share:** 50-75% of project costs
- **Beginning Farmer Cost Share:** Up to **90%** of costs
- **Eligible Projects:**
  - High tunnels
  - Irrigation systems
  - Water management
  - Soil health improvements
  - Conservation practices

**Benefits:**
- Significantly reduced out-of-pocket for infrastructure
- Technical assistance included
- Helps establish sustainable practices early

**How to Apply:**
- Contact local NRCS (Natural Resources Conservation Service) office
- Application periods typically in fall/winter
- Competitive ranking process

### 4E. Pennsylvania Beginning Farmer Tax Credit Program

**Website:** [dced.pa.gov/programs/beginning-farmer-tax-credit-program](https://dced.pa.gov/programs/beginning-farmer-tax-credit-program/)
**Applications:** Open January 1 annually

#### Program Overview

Tax credits for **asset owners** (landowners/equipment owners) who sell or lease to beginning farmers.

**Purpose:** Incentivize established farmers to transition assets to next generation

#### Tax Credits for Asset Owners

**Land Sales:**
- **Standard:** 8% of sale price
- **Limited Land Access Farmer:** 12% of sale price
- **Maximum Credit:** $50,000 per transaction

**Land Leases:**
- **Cash Rent Lease:** 5% of annual rent
- **Crop Share Lease:** 15% of annual rent value
- **Lease Terms:** Must be 2-5 years
- **Maximum Credit:** $50,000 per agreement

#### Beginning Farmer Qualifications (Pennsylvania)

**To qualify as beginning farmer for this program:**

**Residency & Intent:**
- Minnesota resident (or Pennsylvania resident for PA program)
- Seeking entry OR entered farming within last 10 years
- Provide majority of labor and management
- Farm located in state

**Net Worth (Minnesota example for 2025):**
- Cannot exceed $1,042,000
- Adjusted annually

**Education:**
- Enrolled in OR completed approved farm business management program within 10 years of first farming year

**Note:** Each state has specific requirements; verify with state agriculture department

#### Tax Credits for Beginning Farmers

**Minnesota Farm Business Management Tuition Credit:**
- Nonrefundable tax credit equal to FBM tuition paid
- Maximum $1,500 per year
- Available for up to 3 years

**Sources:**
- [Minnesota Beginning Farmer Tax Credit](https://www.mda.state.mn.us/bftc)
- [Beginning Farmer Tax Credit Program | PA DCED](https://dced.pa.gov/programs/beginning-farmer-tax-credit-program/)

## 5. UNIFIED DOCUMENT CHECKLIST

### Master Document List for ALL Loan Applications

This unified checklist covers documents required across Horizon Farm Credit, USDA FSA, and PA Next Gen Farmer loans. Prepare these once and use for multiple applications.

#### 5A. Personal Identification Documents

- [ ] Valid U.S. Passport OR
- [ ] Driver's license + Birth certificate
- [ ] Social Security card or documentation
- [ ] Proof of Pennsylvania residency (for PA programs)
  - [ ] PA driver's license or state ID
  - [ ] Utility bills
  - [ ] Lease/mortgage documents

#### 5B. Citizenship/Legal Status

- [ ] U.S. citizenship proof OR
- [ ] Non-citizen national documentation OR
- [ ] Qualified alien status documentation

#### 5C. Tax Returns (3 Years)

- [ ] Federal tax returns (Form 1040) - Years: 2023, 2024, 2025
- [ ] Schedule F (Profit or Loss from Farming) - All years
- [ ] All schedules and attachments
- [ ] State tax returns (PA) - All years
- [ ] Business tax returns if entity (1065, 1120, 1120-S)

**Organization Tip:** Create folder "Tax_Returns" with subfolders by year

#### 5D. Financial Statements (Current & Historical)

**Current (within 90 days):**
- [ ] Personal balance sheet (assets and liabilities)
- [ ] Farm business balance sheet (if separate entity)
- [ ] Net worth statement
- [ ] Cash on hand documentation

**Three-Year History:**
- [ ] Annual balance sheets (2023, 2024, 2025)
- [ ] Income statements by year
- [ ] Profit & loss statements

**Year-to-Date (current year):**
- [ ] Current income statement
- [ ] Current balance sheet
- [ ] Cash flow statement

#### 5E. Bank Account Documentation

- [ ] 3-6 months bank statements - All personal accounts
- [ ] 3-6 months bank statements - All business accounts
- [ ] Checking account statements
- [ ] Savings account statements
- [ ] Money market accounts

#### 5F. Investment & Asset Documentation

- [ ] Statements for all stocks and bonds
- [ ] Mutual fund statements
- [ ] Retirement account statements (401k, IRA, etc.)
- [ ] Certificate of deposit (CD) statements
- [ ] Current value of all investments
- [ ] Life insurance cash value (if applicable)

#### 5G. Credit & Debt Documentation

**Credit History:**
- [ ] Authorization to pull credit report (signed)
- [ ] Explanation letters for any credit issues
- [ ] Bankruptcy documentation (if applicable - must disclose)

**Existing Debts:**
- [ ] Complete list of all creditors (Creditor List/FSA-2005)
- [ ] Current loan statements for all debts:
  - [ ] Mortgages
  - [ ] Auto loans
  - [ ] Equipment loans
  - [ ] Lines of credit
  - [ ] Credit cards
  - [ ] Personal loans
- [ ] Payment history documentation
- [ ] Payoff amounts (if consolidating)

**Federal Debt:**
- [ ] Certification of no delinquent federal debt
- [ ] IRS payment plan documentation (if applicable)

#### 5H. Farm Experience & Management Documentation

**Experience Verification (Choose applicable):**
- [ ] Certificate: Farm management curriculum completion
  - [ ] Cooperative Extension Service courses
  - [ ] Community college agricultural program
  - [ ] Adult vocational agricultural program
  - [ ] Land Grant university program
- [ ] Documentation: Farm ownership/management for 1+ year
  - [ ] Deed or lease showing dates
  - [ ] Tax returns with Schedule F
  - [ ] Production records
- [ ] Reference letters: Farm labor with leadership role
- [ ] Family farm documentation: Raised on farm with management duties
- [ ] FSA Youth Loan documentation (if applicable)

**For Beginning Farmers:**
- [ ] Timeline of agricultural experience (10-year history)
- [ ] Proof of entering farming within last 10 years
- [ ] Current land ownership documentation (prove <30% county median)
- [ ] Educational transcripts (if using for qualifications)
  - [ ] 16+ credit hours in agricultural field

**Management Certifications:**
- [ ] Ag Biz Masters completion (if using Horizon Farm Credit)
- [ ] SCORE mentorship documentation
- [ ] Other farm business training certificates

#### 5I. Production History (3 Years)

- [ ] Crop production records by year:
  - [ ] Acres planted by crop
  - [ ] Yields per acre
  - [ ] Total production
  - [ ] Sales records
- [ ] Livestock production records:
  - [ ] Number of animals by type
  - [ ] Breeding records
  - [ ] Sales records
  - [ ] Veterinary records
- [ ] Value-added product records (if applicable)
- [ ] Certifications:
  - [ ] Organic certification (if applicable)
  - [ ] GAP (Good Agricultural Practices)
  - [ ] Other relevant certifications

**If New Farmer:**
- [ ] Projected production based on similar operations
- [ ] Market research supporting projections
- [ ] Mentor farmer production data (if available)

#### 5J. Farm Business Plan

**Required Components:**

**Executive Summary:**
- [ ] Farm description and goals
- [ ] Products/services offered
- [ ] Target markets
- [ ] Management team

**Farm Description:**
- [ ] Type of farming operation
- [ ] Location and climate
- [ ] Soil types and land characteristics
- [ ] Current infrastructure
- [ ] Planned infrastructure

**Production Plan:**
- [ ] Crops/livestock to be produced
- [ ] Production methods and practices
- [ ] Rotation plans (if applicable)
- [ ] Timeline by season/year
- [ ] Labor requirements

**Marketing Plan:**
- [ ] Target markets identified
- [ ] Pricing strategy
- [ ] Distribution channels
- [ ] Competitive analysis
- [ ] Sales projections

**Financial Projections:**
- [ ] 3-5 year income projections
- [ ] 3-5 year expense projections
- [ ] Cash flow projections by month (Year 1)
- [ ] Cash flow projections by year (Years 2-5)
- [ ] Break-even analysis
- [ ] Sensitivity analysis (best/worst case scenarios)

**Risk Management:**

## 7. APPLICATION TIMELINE & PROCESS COMPARISON

### 7A. Horizon Farm Credit

**Estimated Timeline:** 4-8 weeks

**Process Steps:**
1. **Initial Contact:** Call or email (888.339.3334 / info@horizonfc.com)
2. **Initial Consultation:** 1 week - Meet with loan officer
3. **Application Preparation:** 2-3 weeks - Gather documents
4. **Application Submission:** Submit complete package
5. **Underwriting:** 2-4 weeks - Review and analysis
6. **Approval Decision:** Notification of decision
7. **Closing:** 1-2 weeks - Finalize loan

**Tips for Success:**
- Build relationship with loan officer early
- Prepare comprehensive business plan
- Have all financial documents organized
- Be responsive to requests for additional information
- Consider FarmStarter Microloan if limited credit history

---

### 7B. USDA FSA Loans

**Estimated Timeline:** 6-12 weeks (can vary significantly)

**Process Steps:**
1. **Contact FSA Office:** Schedule appointment
2. **Initial Meeting:** 1-2 weeks - Meet with Farm Loan Officer/Manager
3. **Application Package Assembly:** 3-4 weeks - Complete FSA forms
4. **Application Submission:** Submit complete application
5. **FSA Review:** 4-6 weeks:
   - Eligibility verification
   - Credit check
   - Financial analysis
   - Property appraisal (if real estate)
6. **Approval Decision:** Loan committee review
7. **Closing:** 2-3 weeks - Complete loan documents

**FSA-Specific Considerations:**
- Use Loan Assistance Tool at farmers.gov to check eligibility first
- FSA Direct loans have limited funds - apply early in fiscal year (October-December preferred)
- Microloans process faster than standard loans
- Beginning farmer status may expedite
- Be patient - government process takes longer but rates are competitive

**Priority Funding:**
- Beginning farmers get priority consideration
- FSA sets aside funds specifically for beginning farmers
- Apply as early as possible in fiscal year

---

### 7C. PA Next Generation Farmer Loan

**Estimated Timeline:** 8-12 weeks

**Process Steps:**
1. **Verify Eligibility:** Confirm beginning farmer status and net worth
2. **Contact Approved Lender:** 1 week - Initial discussion with lender (EDC Finance or approved institution)
3. **PA Single Application:** 1-2 weeks - Complete online application at pa.gov
4. **Lender Application:** 2-3 weeks - Complete lender's application process
5. **Underwriting:** 4-6 weeks:
   - Commercial lender underwrites loan
   - DCED reviews for program eligibility
   - Property appraisal (if land purchase)
6. **Dual Approval:** Both lender and DCED must approve
7. **Tax-Exempt Bond Processing:** 2-3 weeks - State processes bond
8. **Closing:** Complete loan through lender

**PA Program Considerations:**
- Must use approved lender (not all lenders participate)
- Dual approval process adds time
- Net worth limits adjusted annually - verify current limits
- Tax-exempt bond structure creates extra administrative steps
- 15-25 year terms for real estate make payments manageable

---

### 7D. Multiple Application Strategy

**Applying to Multiple Programs Simultaneously:**

**Advantages:**
- Maximizes chances of approval
- Allows comparison of terms and rates
- Creates negotiating leverage
- Backup options if one declines

**Recommended Approach:**

**Phase 1: Preparation (Weeks 1-3)**
- Gather all documents from unified checklist
- Create master file system (digital and physical)
- Develop comprehensive business plan
- Organize financial data

**Phase 2: Initial Applications (Weeks 4-5)**
- Submit FSA application first (longest timeline)
- Submit Horizon Farm Credit application

**Phase 3: Manage Process (Weeks 6-12)**
- Respond promptly to all requests
- Keep all lenders informed (don't hide multiple applications)
- Maintain communication log
- Update documents as needed

**Phase 4: Decision & Selection (Weeks 10-14)**
- Compare offers:
  - Interest rates
  - Terms and payment schedules
  - Fees and closing costs
  - Flexibility and prepayment options
  - Lender relationship and support
- Negotiate final terms
- Select best option(s)
- Politely decline others

**Blending Strategy:**
- FSA loans can be combined with conventional loans
- Example: FSA Down Payment Loan (45%) + PA Next Gen (45%) + Your down payment (10%)
- Consult with lenders about blending options
