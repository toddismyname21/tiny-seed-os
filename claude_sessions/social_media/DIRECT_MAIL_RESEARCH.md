## Executive Summary

Direct mail remains highly effective for local farm marketing with **2.7-4.4% response rates** (vs 0.12% for email). For Tiny Seed Farm, **Every Door Direct Mail (EDDM)** is the recommended starting point - it's affordable, requires no mailing list, and targets by geography.

---

## USPS Program Options

### 1. Every Door Direct Mail (EDDM) - **RECOMMENDED FOR FIRST CAMPAIGN**

**What it is:** Deliver postcards/flyers to every address on selected mail carrier routes without needing names or addresses.

**Best for:** Local businesses targeting neighborhoods, farms targeting nearby communities

| Feature | EDDM Retail | EDDM BMEU |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Postage Rate | $0.247/piece | $0.242-$0.291/piece |
| Minimum | 200 pieces | 200 pieces |
| Maximum | 5,000/day/ZIP | Unlimited |
| Requirements | Self-service, drop at post office | Business mail account needed |
| Addressing | "Postal Customer" - no addresses needed | Same |

**Size Requirements:**
- Minimum: 6.125" x 11.5" (oversized postcard)
- Maximum: 12" x 15"
- Weight: 3.3 oz or less

**Pros:**
- No mailing list needed
- Lowest cost per piece
- Target specific neighborhoods
- Simple to execute

**Cons:**
- No personalization (everyone gets same piece)
- Can't exclude existing customers
- Must be oversized format

### 2. USPS Marketing Mail (Bulk Mail with Addresses)

**What it is:** Send to a purchased or owned mailing list at bulk rates.

| Postcard Size | Rate |
|---------------|------|
| Standard (4.25" x 6") | $0.355-$0.439/piece |
| Oversized (6" x 9"+) | $0.40-$0.50/piece |

**Requirements:**
- Minimum 200 pieces OR 50 lbs
- Must be presorted by ZIP
- Need mailing list with addresses

**Pros:**
- Can personalize by name
- Can target specific demographics
- Can exclude existing customers

**Cons:**
- Need to purchase/build mailing list
- More complex to execute
- Slightly higher cost

### 3. First-Class Mail

**Rates:** $0.61/postcard, $0.73/letter

**Best for:** Follow-up mailings, thank you cards, small batches under 200

## Response Rate Expectations

### Industry Benchmarks

| Metric | Rate |
|--------|------|
| Average direct mail response | 2.7-4.4% |
| Retail/local business | 2.96-3.09% |
| With personalization (name) | Up to 44% lift |
| New movers | 5x more likely to convert |

### For Tiny Seed Farm (Estimated)

| Scenario | Response Rate | Responses per 1,000 |
|----------|---------------|---------------------|
| Cold EDDM (no targeting) | 1-2% | 10-20 |
| Targeted neighborhoods | 2-3% | 20-30 |
| New movers list | 3-5% | 30-50 |
| With strong offer | 4-6% | 40-60 |

### ROI Calculation

**Assumptions:**
- 1,000 pieces mailed
- Cost: $500
- Response rate: 3% (30 responses)
- Conversion to CSA: 33% (10 new members)
- CSA value: $500/season

**Result:**
- Revenue: 10 x $500 = $5,000
- Cost: $500
- **ROI: 10x** ($5,000 / $500)

Even at 1% response with 10% conversion:
- 10 responses → 1 CSA member → $500 revenue
- **ROI: 1x** (break even)

## Best Practices for Farm Direct Mail

### Design Tips

1. **Lead with a compelling image** - Beautiful farm photo, fresh produce, harvest scene
2. **Clear headline** - "Fresh From Your Neighbor's Farm" or "Join Our Farm Family"
3. **Simple offer** - "First CSA Box 50% Off" or "Free Farm Tour"
4. **Easy response** - QR code, short URL, phone number
5. **Urgency** - "Limited spots" or "Spring signup deadline"

### Timing

| Season | Campaign Focus |
|--------|----------------|
| January-February | CSA signup push |
| March-April | Spring planting, farm tours |
| May-June | Peak season, farmer's market promotion |
| September-October | Fall harvest, holiday pre-orders |
| November | Thanksgiving, winter CSA |

### Tracking Methods

1. **Unique URL:** tinyseedfarm.com/neighbor
2. **QR Code:** Links to landing page with tracking
3. **Promo Code:** "NEIGHBOR25" for 25% off
4. **Dedicated phone:** Track calls from mailer
5. **"How did you hear about us?"** on signup form
