## Disputed Expenses - Capital vs Maintenance

**Per Don's 2023 Draft Lease (Section 8.1):**
> "Capital costs for repair to buildings and equipment are borne by the **lessor** [Don]; maintenance costs by the **lessee** [Todd]."

**Definition from lease:**
> "Depreciable improvements which result in a betterment to the property, restores the property, or adapts it to a new or different use, would be treated as an improvement and the cost borne by the lessor."

- This benefits any future tenant, not just Todd

**Verdict: CAPITAL EXPENSE - Don's responsibility**

### Expense 2: Heater That Didn't Work

| Factor | Analysis |
|--------|----------|
| What happened | Heater non-functional when Todd started using it |
| Who paid | Todd ($450) |

- A pre-existing defect is the landlord's responsibility
- "Maintenance" means keeping working equipment working
- "Repair" of broken equipment = capital expense per the lease

**Verdict: CAPITAL EXPENSE - Don's responsibility**

| Factor | Analysis |

**From Don's lease:**
