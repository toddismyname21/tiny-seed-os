## ARTICLE 1: PURPOSE

The purpose of this lease is to allow use of land, water and forest resources, buildings, machinery, and equipment of the Kretschmann Farm for the production of organic produce while maintaining the permanent productivity of the soil, the quality and quantity of water resources, the long-term maintenance of buildings, and the reasonable lifetime of all machinery, tools, and equipment.

In the case of the land, among other considerations, this includes soil health, organic matter content, erosion control, biodiversity, minimization of compaction, and maximizing living cover.

### 2.1 Land

## ARTICLE 3: TERM

### 3.1 Initial Term
**CHANGE FROM 2023 DRAFT:** This lease shall be for an initial term of **five (5) years**, commencing January 1, 2026, and ending December 31, 2030, unless terminated earlier according to the provisions below.

### 3.2 Renewal
This lease shall automatically renew for successive **three (3) year** terms unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least **twelve (12) months** prior to the end of the current term.

### 3.3 Intent
Both parties acknowledge that the Landlord has publicly expressed intent to place this property into a community farmland trust with long-term agricultural leasehold rights. This lease shall remain in effect during any transition to such trust structure, with terms to be renegotiated in good faith at that time.

---

## ARTICLE 4: RENT AND PAYMENT

### 4.1 Land Rent
Lessee agrees to pay **$500 per acre per year** for land actively cropped. For 2026, this equals **$3,000** for 6 acres.

### 4.2 Infrastructure and Equipment Rent
Lessee agrees to pay infrastructure and equipment rental per the formulas established in Exhibit B and Exhibit C, calculated as:
- (Asset Valuation / Useful Life) + (5% of Valuation for maintenance) Ã— (Usage Percentage)

Estimated annual infrastructure/equipment rent: **$11,812** (per 2025 calculations, subject to annual adjustment).

### 4.3 Additional Costs
- Custom tractor work: $100/hour
- Fuel allocation: Actual usage per fuel logs
- Portable toilet: 75% of rental cost
- Utilities: Fair apportionment per usage

### 4.4 Payment Schedule
Rent shall be paid **quarterly** at the beginning of each quarter (January 1, April 1, July 1, October 1).

### 4.5 Late Payment
A late penalty of 1% per month may be assessed on payments more than 30 days overdue.

### 4.6 Annual Review
Rental rates may be renegotiated annually by mutual agreement, with any changes effective January 1 of the following year.

---

## ARTICLE 5: SECURITY DEPOSIT

Lessee shall pay a security deposit of **$1,000** to be held by Lessor and applied to:
- Return of any lands not left in perennial legume cover
- Repair or return of buildings/equipment to good condition
- Any outstanding rent at termination

Remainder to be returned within 60 days of lease termination.

---

## ARTICLE 6: PERMITTED AND PROHIBITED USES

### 6.1 Permitted Uses
- Organic crop production certified under the National Organic Program
- Harvest, preparation for sale, and storage of crops
- Normal use of tractors and equipment for production
- Routine maintenance of buildings and equipment
- On-farm sales and CSA distribution
- Hosting farm tours and educational events

### 6.2 Prohibited Uses (without written consent)
- Agricultural practices not certifiable as organic
- Sale or transfer of water from the farm
- Land disposal or burning of toxic/synthetic substances
- Animal husbandry (cattle, hogs, poultry)
- Subletting to third parties

## ARTICLE 7: LESSOR'S OBLIGATIONS

Lessor agrees to:

7.1 Provide a portable toilet (cost split with Lessee)

7.2 Provide non-exclusive access to tools and equipment per the Equipment Schedule (Exhibit C)

7.3 Clearly identify facilities, tools, and equipment covered by this lease

7.4 Fairly apportion utility and fuel costs among multiple users

7.5 Maintain log of custom work performed for Lessee

7.6 Provide training on specific equipment at agreed-upon rates

7.7 **NEW:** Provide written notice of any potential sale, transfer, or encumbrance of the property at least **12 months** in advance

7.8 **NEW:** Support Lessee's continued tenancy in any land trust transition negotiations

## ARTICLE 8: LESSEE'S OBLIGATIONS

Lessee agrees to:

8.1 Prepare an annual management/crop plan for Landlord review by January 15

8.2 Complete annual soil testing and apply amendments as needed

8.3 Maintain soil health and fertility at or above baseline levels

8.4 Prevent establishment of noxious weeds

8.5 Maintain mowed roadways and areas around farm buildings

8.6 Clearly mark all Lessee-owned tools and equipment

8.7 Not leave equipment exposed to weather

8.8 Use equipment only as intended and in compliance with applicable laws

8.9 Repair or replace equipment damaged through negligence

8.10 Remove trash and waste when exceeding 55 gallons

8.11 Compost all crop residues per NOP rules

8.12 Plant winter cover crops in all cropped fields by November 1

8.13 Remove plastic mulch from fields by season's end

8.14 Keep leased items on premises unless agreed otherwise

8.15 Submit required documents per Exhibit D (Documentation Requirements)

8.16 Share equitably in utility costs

8.17 Maintain liability insurance and provide proof to Lessor

## ARTICLE 9: CAPITAL IMPROVEMENTS

### 9.1 Maintenance vs. Capital
- **Capital repairs** (betterment, restoration, adaptation): Lessor responsibility
- **Routine maintenance** (cleaning, lubrication, inspection): Lessee responsibility

### 9.2 Removable Improvements
Lessee may make minor temporary improvements with written approval. Lessee may remove such improvements at lease termination, restoring the area to previous condition.

### 9.3 Permanent Improvements
Permanent improvements require Lessor's written approval. At termination, Lessor shall compensate Lessee at fair market value minus depreciation.

### 9.4 **NEW: Investment Documentation**
Lessee shall maintain records of all improvements, amendments, and investments made to the property. In the event of early termination by Lessor, Lessor shall reimburse Lessee for:
- Unamortized value of approved permanent improvements
- Cost of soil amendments applied in current season
- Value of unharvested crops at fair market value

---

## ARTICLE 10: TERMINATION

### 10.1 Notice Period
**CHANGE FROM 2023 DRAFT:** Either party may terminate this lease with **twelve (12) months** written notice to the other party (changed from 2 months in original draft).

### 10.2 Termination by Lessor for Cause
Lessor may terminate with 60 days notice for:
- Non-payment of rent for 60+ days
- Material breach of lease terms not cured within 30 days of written notice
- Loss of organic certification due to Lessee actions

### 10.3 Termination by Lessee
Lessee may terminate with 6 months notice for any reason.

### 10.4 End of Term Obligations
At termination, Lessee shall:
- Remove all Lessee-owned property
- Leave all land in perennial legume cover or actively cropped condition
- Return all equipment in good working order
- Provide all records and documentation to Lessor

---

## ARTICLE 11: RIGHT OF FIRST REFUSAL

### 11.1 **NEW: Sale of Property**
If Lessor receives a bona fide offer to purchase all or part of the leased premises and intends to accept, Lessor shall first offer Lessee the right to purchase on the same terms and conditions.

### 11.2 **NEW: Response Period**
Lessee shall have **90 days** from receipt of written notice to accept or decline the offer. If Lessee declines or fails to respond, Lessor may proceed with the sale.

### 11.3 **NEW: Lease Continuity**
Any sale of the property shall be subject to this lease. Purchaser shall assume all Lessor obligations under this agreement.

---

## ARTICLE 12: ACCESS AND OPERATIONS

### 12.1 Hours
Operations permitted 6:00 AM to 10:00 PM, Monday through Saturday. Sunday work requires advance notice.

### 12.2 Vehicles
Maximum 5 vehicles on premises; maximum 3 parked near barn/house.

### 12.3 Coordination
Lessee shall coordinate with other lessees on shared facilities and equipment.

### 12.4 Lessor Access
Lessor retains right to access property for inspection without prior notification.

---

## ARTICLE 13: INSURANCE AND INDEMNIFICATION

### 13.1 Insurance
Lessee shall maintain general liability insurance of at least $1,000,000 per occurrence and provide proof annually to Lessor.

### 13.2 Indemnification
Lessee shall indemnify Lessor for damage exceeding normal wear and tear, and for damage caused to other lessees' crops or equipment.

---

## ARTICLE 14: DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### 14.1 Default and Cure
A default may be cured upon written notice within 14 days of receipt.

### 14.2 Mediation
Any disputes shall first be submitted to mediation before legal action.

### 14.3 Governing Law
This agreement shall be governed by the laws of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania.

---

## ARTICLE 15: GENERAL PROVISIONS

15.1 This lease may be amended only by written agreement signed by both parties.

15.2 Lessee shall not assign or sublease without written consent of Lessor.

15.3 Notices shall be delivered in writing to the addresses above.

15.4 If any provision is found unenforceable, remaining provisions shall remain in effect.
