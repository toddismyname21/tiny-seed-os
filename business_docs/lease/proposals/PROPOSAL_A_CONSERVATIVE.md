# PROPOSAL A: Conservative/Protective Lease
## Maximum Security for Todd

**Philosophy:** This is what a lawyer would recommend. It prioritizes protecting Todd's investments, providing long-term security, and ensuring legal enforceability. It assumes the relationship could become adversarial.

---

## RATIONALE

### Why This Approach?

Todd has operated for 10+ years without a signed lease. He has:
- Invested tens of thousands of dollars in soil amendments
- Built customer relationships worth hundreds of thousands in lifetime value
- Installed improvements on Don's property (greenhouse plastic, repairs)
- Stored his own equipment (tunnels worth $15,000-30,000+) waiting for permission

**Without legal protection, Todd could lose everything with 60 days notice.**

This proposal treats the arrangement as a business transaction requiring formal protections, not a handshake deal between friends.

### AGRICULTURAL LAND LEASE AGREEMENT

**This Agreement** is entered into as of _____________, 2026, by and between:

**LESSOR:** Donald Kretschmann, Revocable Trust, 257 Zeigler Rd., Rochester, PA 15074

**LESSEE:** Todd Wilson, d/b/a Tiny Seed Farm

### ARTICLE 1: LEASED PREMISES

**1.1 Land:** Approximately ten (10) acres of certified organic farmland at the Kretschmann Farm property, as depicted in Exhibit A.

**1.2 Structures:** Access to greenhouse (27' x 96'), high tunnel (21' x 96'), barn space (4,250 sq ft), quonset structure (75% usage), and walk-in coolers per Exhibit B.

**1.3 Equipment:** Non-exclusive use of tractors, implements, and tools per Equipment Schedule (Exhibit C).

---

### ARTICLE 2: TERM AND RENEWAL

**2.1 Initial Term:** This lease shall be for an initial term of **ten (10) years**, commencing January 1, 2026, and ending December 31, 2035.

**2.2 Automatic Renewal:** This lease shall automatically renew for successive **five (5) year** terms unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least **twenty-four (24) months** prior to the end of the current term.

**2.3 No Early Termination by Lessor:** Lessor may not terminate this lease prior to the end of any term except for material breach as defined in Article 11. Material breach requires:
- Written notice of specific breach
- 90-day cure period
- Opportunity for mediation before termination

**2.4 Early Termination by Lessee:** Lessee may terminate with 12 months written notice.

---

### ARTICLE 3: RENT

**3.1 Base Rent:** Lessee agrees to pay the following annual rent:

| Category | Annual Amount |
|----------|---------------|
| Land (10 acres Ã— $500/acre) | $5,000 |
| Infrastructure | $4,812 |
| Equipment | $7,000 |
| **Total Annual Rent** | **$16,812** |

**3.2 Payment Schedule:** Rent shall be paid quarterly in advance on January 1, April 1, July 1, and October 1.

**3.3 Rent Increase Cap:** Annual rent increases shall not exceed **3%** without Lessee's written consent. Any increase shall be communicated in writing at least 90 days before the start of the year.

**3.4 Rent Credit for Capital Improvements:** Lessee shall receive rent credits for any capital improvements made to Lessor's property, as documented in Article 7.

---

### ARTICLE 4: MIGRANT WORKER HOUSING

**4.1 Housing Lease:** Lessee shall have the option to lease migrant worker housing at $1,000/month, separately documented.

**4.2 Housing Not Required:** Housing is optional and may be terminated by Lessee with 60 days notice without affecting the farm lease.

---

### ARTICLE 5: UTILITIES

**5.1 Electric:** Lessee shall pay actual metered usage. If no separate meter exists, Lessee shall pay a fair allocation not to exceed **10%** of total property electric unless usage demonstrably exceeds this amount.

**5.2 Dispute Resolution:** Any dispute over utility allocation shall be resolved by independent audit at Lessor's expense.

---

### ARTICLE 6: LESSEE'S INFRASTRUCTURE RIGHTS

**6.1 Right to Install Lessee-Owned Equipment:** Lessee is hereby granted permission to install the following Lessee-owned infrastructure on the premises:
- One (1) high tunnel, 30' x 100'
- Four (4) caterpillar tunnels, 15' x 90' each
- One (1) propagation house

**6.2 Removal Rights:** All Lessee-owned infrastructure may be removed by Lessee at any time, including upon termination of this lease.

**6.3 No Rent for Lessee's Infrastructure:** Lessee shall not pay rent on infrastructure owned by Lessee.

**6.4 Location Approval:** Lessor shall approve locations for Lessee infrastructure within 30 days of request. Approval shall not be unreasonably withheld.

---

### ARTICLE 7: CAPITAL IMPROVEMENTS AND INVESTMENT PROTECTION

**7.1 Definition of Capital Improvements:** Capital improvements are permanent or semi-permanent improvements that enhance the value of Lessor's property, including but not limited to:
- Greenhouse plastic replacement
- Heater/cooler repairs
- Irrigation installation
- Soil amendments
- Tree removal
- Building repairs

**7.2 Lessor's Responsibility:** Per industry standard, capital repairs to Lessor's property are Lessor's responsibility. If Lessee performs capital repairs, Lessee shall receive:
- Full reimbursement within 60 days, OR
- Rent credit equal to 110% of documented costs

**7.3 Investment Documentation:** Lessee shall maintain a log of all improvements, amendments, and investments made to the property with dated receipts and photographs.

**7.4 Reimbursement Upon Termination:** If Lessor terminates this lease for any reason other than Lessee's material breach, Lessor shall reimburse Lessee for:

| Investment Type | Reimbursement |
|-----------------|---------------|
| Unamortized improvements | 100% of cost minus depreciation |
| Soil amendments (current year) | 100% of cost |
| Soil amendments (previous 2 years) | 50% of cost |
| Unharvested crops | Fair market value |
| Moving costs | Actual documented costs up to $10,000 |

**7.5 Improvement Schedule:** Attached as Exhibit D is a schedule of all improvements made by Lessee prior to this lease, with agreed values.

---

### ARTICLE 8: RIGHT OF FIRST REFUSAL

**8.1 Sale of Property:** If Lessor receives a bona fide offer to purchase all or any portion of the leased premises and intends to accept, Lessor shall first offer Lessee the right to purchase on the same terms and conditions.

**8.2 Notice Requirements:** Lessor shall provide written notice including:
- Complete terms of the offer
- Identity of the prospective purchaser
- Closing timeline

**8.3 Response Period:** Lessee shall have **one hundred eighty (180) days** from receipt of written notice to:
- Accept the offer on same terms
- Propose alternative terms for negotiation
- Decline in writing

**8.4 Financing Period:** If Lessee accepts, Lessee shall have an additional 120 days to secure financing.

**8.5 Waiver:** If Lessee declines or fails to respond, Lessor may proceed with the sale, but the purchaser shall be bound by this lease.

---

### ARTICLE 9: LEASE SURVIVES TRANSFER

**9.1 Binding on Successors:** This lease shall be binding upon and inure to the benefit of the parties and their respective heirs, executors, administrators, successors, and assigns.

**9.2 Sale Subject to Lease:** Any sale, transfer, gift, or other conveyance of the property shall be subject to this lease. Purchaser shall assume all Lessor obligations.

**9.3 Death of Lessor:** Upon Lessor's death, this lease shall continue in full force with Lessor's estate, heirs, or successors.

**9.4 Notification of Transfer:** Lessor shall provide Lessee with at least **twelve (12) months** advance written notice of any intended sale, transfer, or encumbrance of the property.

---

### ARTICLE 10: MAINTENANCE RESPONSIBILITIES

**10.1 Lessee Responsibilities (Maintenance):**
- Routine cleaning and inspection
- Lubrication and minor adjustments
- Consumables (oil, filters, blades)
- Mowing around buildings
- Cover crop planting

**10.2 Lessor Responsibilities (Capital):**
- Structural repairs to buildings
- Equipment repairs exceeding $200
- Refrigeration equipment repairs
- Tree removal
- Well and water system maintenance
- Electrical system repairs

---

### ARTICLE 11: DEFAULT AND REMEDIES

**11.1 Material Breach by Lessee:** Material breach includes:
- Non-payment of rent for 90+ days
- Loss of organic certification due to Lessee's actions
- Abandonment of property for 6+ months

**11.2 Cure Period:** Lessee shall have 90 days to cure any breach after written notice.

**11.3 Material Breach by Lessor:** Material breach includes:
- Failure to make required capital repairs
- Interference with Lessee's operations
- Failure to honor right of first refusal
- Unauthorized entry or harassment

**11.4 Lessee's Remedies:** If Lessor breaches, Lessee may:
- Cure the breach and deduct from rent
- Seek specific performance
- Terminate with full reimbursement per Article 7.4

---

### ARTICLE 12: DISPUTE RESOLUTION

**12.1 Mediation:** Any dispute shall first be submitted to non-binding mediation within 30 days.

**12.2 Arbitration:** If mediation fails, disputes shall be resolved by binding arbitration under the rules of the American Arbitration Association.

**12.3 Governing Law:** This agreement shall be governed by the laws of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania.

**12.4 Attorneys' Fees:** The prevailing party shall be entitled to reasonable attorneys' fees.

---

### ARTICLE 13: INSURANCE AND INDEMNIFICATION

**13.1 Lessee Insurance:** Lessee shall maintain general liability insurance of $1,000,000 per occurrence.

**13.2 Lessor Insurance:** Lessor shall maintain property insurance covering all structures and equipment included in this lease.

---

### ARTICLE 14: GENERAL PROVISIONS

**14.1 Entire Agreement:** This lease constitutes the entire agreement and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, or agreements.

**14.2 Amendment:** This lease may only be amended in writing signed by both parties.

**14.3 Severability:** If any provision is unenforceable, remaining provisions remain in effect.

**14.4 Recording:** Lessee may record a memorandum of this lease with the Beaver County Recorder of Deeds.

| Right of first refusal (180 days) | Real opportunity to purchase |

## TALKING POINTS FOR TODD

### Opening the Conversation

> "Don, I've been farming here for over 10 years and I want to be here for another 30. But I'm at a point where I need to make big investments - my tunnels, more infrastructure, maybe buying some of your equipment eventually. I can't make those investments without knowing I have security. Can we finally formalize our arrangement?"

### If Don Pushes Back on Length

> "I know 10 years sounds like a long time, but for a farmer it's not. I need at least two full crop rotations to see returns on soil investments. And perennials like asparagus or berries take 3-4 years before they even produce. Without a long-term commitment, I can't grow the farm the way we both want."

### If Don Pushes Back on Right of First Refusal

> "I know you've said you want to put this into a land trust. This right of first refusal protects both of us - it gives me the chance to stay if your plans change, and it shows any future buyer that there's a committed farmer here. That actually makes the land more valuable for a land trust, not less."

### If Don Says "You Don't Trust Me"

> "It's not about trust, Don. It's about what happens if something happens to you. Your family might not share your vision. A signed lease protects your legacy - it guarantees this land stays in the hands of someone who cares about organic farming, not a developer."

### On Investment Reimbursement

> "I just replaced all the greenhouse plastic - that was $X and 40 hours of labor. I've been paying for repairs that are really capital improvements to your property. The lease you drafted in 2023 said capital expenses are the landlord's responsibility. I'm just asking us to follow your own terms."
