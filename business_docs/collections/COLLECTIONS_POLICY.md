# Collections & Accounts Receivable Policy
## Tiny Seed Farm - Wholesale Credit Terms

**Effective Date:** January 2026
**Purpose:** Prevent future situations like Alberta's Food Group ($6,045.50 overdue)

---

## THE PROBLEM THIS SOLVES

Alberta's Food Group accumulated $6,045.50 in unpaid invoices over 16 months before any formal collection action was taken. This policy ensures:

1. Clear expectations upfront
2. Early warning when accounts go overdue
3. Systematic escalation process
4. Legal protection if collection is needed

---

## CREDIT TIERS

### Tier 1: Prepay Only (New Accounts)
**Applies to:** All new wholesale accounts for first 3 months

- Payment required before or upon delivery
- No invoicing, no credit terms
- After 3 months of consistent orders and on-time payment, may upgrade to Tier 2

### Tier 2: Net 14 (Established Accounts)
**Applies to:** Accounts with 3+ months good history

- Invoice upon delivery
- Payment due within 14 days
- Credit limit: $500
- After 6 months of on-time payment, may upgrade to Tier 3

### Tier 3: Net 30 (Trusted Accounts)
**Applies to:** Accounts with 6+ months perfect payment history

- Invoice upon delivery
- Payment due within 30 days
- Credit limit: $1,500
- Must maintain on-time payment to keep this status

## INVOICING PROCESS

### Upon Delivery
1. Create invoice in QuickBooks
2. Email invoice to customer same day
3. Include clear payment terms on invoice
4. Note due date prominently

### Invoice Must Include
- Invoice number
- Invoice date
- Due date (bold)
- Itemized products with prices
- Total amount due
- Payment methods accepted
- Late payment policy

## COLLECTION ESCALATION PROCESS

### Day 1-14: Current
- Invoice sent upon delivery
- No action needed

### Day 15: First Reminder (Tier 2) / Day 31: First Reminder (Tier 3)
**Trigger:** Invoice 1 day past due

**Action:**
- Send friendly reminder email
- Subject: "Friendly Reminder: Invoice #[XXX] Due"
- Tone: Helpful, assumes oversight

**Template:**
```
Hi [Name],

Just a quick reminder that Invoice #[XXX] for $[Amount] was due on [Date].

You can pay online at [link] or mail a check to our farm address.

Let me know if you have any questions!

Thanks,
Todd
```

### Day 21 (Tier 2) / Day 45 (Tier 3): Second Notice
**Trigger:** Invoice 7+ days past due (Tier 2) or 14+ days (Tier 3)

**Action:**
- Send firmer reminder
- Phone call if no response to email
- Note account in AR tracking

**Template:**
```
Hi [Name],

This is a second notice regarding Invoice #[XXX] for $[Amount], which is now [X] days past due.

Please remit payment immediately or contact me to discuss if there's an issue.

Per our credit terms, a 1.5% monthly late fee will be applied to overdue balances.

Thanks,
Todd
```

### Day 30 (Tier 2) / Day 60 (Tier 3): Final Notice Before Suspension
**Trigger:** Invoice 14+ days past due (Tier 2) or 30+ days (Tier 3)

**Action:**
- Send final notice via email AND postal mail
- Suspend future deliveries until payment received
- Downgrade credit tier

**Template:**
```
[Name],

Invoice #[XXX] for $[Amount] is now [X] days past due. Despite previous reminders, we have not received payment or communication from you.

Effective immediately:
- Your account is SUSPENDED
- No further deliveries will be made until the balance is paid
- Your credit terms have been revoked

Please pay the full amount due within 7 days to avoid further action.

If you are experiencing financial difficulty, please contact me immediately to discuss a payment plan.

Todd Wilson
Tiny Seed Farm
```

### Day 45 (Tier 2) / Day 75 (Tier 3): Formal Demand
**Trigger:** Invoice 30+ days past due (Tier 2) or 45+ days (Tier 3)

**Action:**
- Send formal demand letter via certified mail
- Include payment agreement for signature
- Set 10-day deadline

**Use templates from:** `/business_docs/collections/ALBERTAS_FINAL_DEMAND_LETTER.md`

### Day 60+ (Tier 2) / Day 90+ (Tier 3): Legal Action
**Trigger:** No response to formal demand

**Action:**
- File claim in Magisterial District Court (Small Claims)
- Or turn over to collections agency

## SUSPENSION & REINSTATEMENT

### Automatic Suspension Triggers
- Any invoice 30+ days past due
- Two invoices past due simultaneously
- Bounced check or failed payment
- No response to collection attempts

### Reinstatement Requirements
1. Pay all outstanding balance in full
2. Sign new credit agreement
3. Downgrade to Tier 1 (prepay) for minimum 3 months
4. Demonstrate 3 months on-time payment before credit restored

---

## WRITE-OFF POLICY

### When to Write Off
- After all collection efforts exhausted
- Court judgment uncollectable
- Customer out of business
- Cost of collection exceeds balance

### Write-Off Process
1. Document all collection attempts
2. Owner approval required
3. Record as bad debt expense
4. Keep records for 7 years (tax purposes)
5. May still report to credit bureaus

## LESSONS FROM ALBERTA'S

| What Went Wrong | Policy Fix |
|-----------------|------------|
| No credit application | Now required for all accounts |
| Continued deliveries while overdue | Auto-suspend at 30 days |
| No systematic follow-up | Weekly AR review + escalation |
| Waited 16 months to act | Maximum 90 days to legal action |
| No written payment terms | Credit agreement required |

**Alberta's cost us:**
- $6,045.50 in outstanding invoices
- Overdraft fees
- Short-term borrowing costs
- Management time
- Stress

**This policy ensures it doesn't happen again.**
