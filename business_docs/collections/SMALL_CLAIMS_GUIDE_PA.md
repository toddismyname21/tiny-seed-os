## STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS

### Step 1: Find the Correct Court

**Options for where to file:**
- Where the defendant (Alberta's) is located
- Where the contract was signed
- Where the work was performed (deliveries made)

**To find your court:**
1. Go to: https://www.pacourts.us/courts/magisterial-district-courts
2. Use the "District Court Finder"
3. Enter Alberta's business address
4. This will show the correct Magisterial District Court

**Beaver County Magisterial District Courts:**
- Call the court to confirm jurisdiction
- Ask about current filing fees
- Ask about hearing timeline

### Step 2: Get the Forms

**Required Form:** AOPC 310A - MDJ Civil Complaint

**Download from:** https://www.pacourts.us/forms/for-the-public
- Look under "Magisterial District Judge" section
- Find "Civil Complaint"

**Also download:**
- Confidential Information Form (if including sensitive info)

### Step 5: File the Complaint

**What to bring to the courthouse:**
- Completed complaint form (original + copies)
- Filing fee
- Your ID
- Alberta's business address for service

**The clerk will:**
- Accept your filing
- Assign a case number
- Schedule a hearing (usually 12-60 days out)
- Arrange for service on the defendant

### Step 6: Service of Process

The court will serve Alberta's Food Group via:
- Certified mail (return receipt), OR
- Personal service by constable

**You don't have to do this yourself.** The court handles it.

### Step 7: Prepare for the Hearing

**What to bring:**

| Item | Purpose | Copies |
|------|---------|--------|
| All 31 invoices | Proves debt | 3 sets |
| Delivery records | Proves goods delivered | 3 sets |
| Email - Dec 9, 2025 | Proves demand made | 3 sets |
| Email - Dec 12, 2025 | Proves second demand | 3 sets |
| Final demand letter | Proves formal demand | 3 sets |
| Payment Agreement sent | Shows attempt to resolve | 3 sets |
| Record of $300 payment | Shows partial payment | 3 sets |
| This guide/notes | For your reference | 1 set |

**Why 3 copies?** One for you, one for the judge, one for the defendant.

### Step 8: The Hearing

**What happens:**
1. Judge calls the case
2. You (plaintiff) present your case first
3. Explain what happened: deliveries made, invoices sent, payment demanded, no response
4. Show your evidence
5. Defendant responds
6. Judge asks questions
7. Judge may decide immediately or mail decision

**Tips:**
- Be calm and professional
- Stick to facts, not emotions
- Refer to specific invoices and dates
- Don't interrupt the defendant
- Answer the judge's questions directly

**What to say (opening statement):**
```
Your Honor, I'm Todd Wilson, owner of Tiny Seed Farm. Between
September 2024 and October 2025, I delivered vegetable products
to Alberta's Food Group totaling $6,045.50 over 31 separate
invoices. They made one payment of $300, leaving $5,745.50
unpaid. I sent formal demands on December 9 and December 12,
2025, and a final demand with a payment agreement on [date].
I received no response. I'm asking the court to order payment
of the $5,745.50 owed plus my court costs.
```

### Step 9: After the Judgment

**If you win:**
- Defendant has 30 days to pay or appeal
- If they don't pay, you can pursue collection

**Collection options after judgment:**
- Wage garnishment
- Bank account levy
- Property lien
- Credit bureau reporting

**If you lose:**
- You can appeal to the Court of Common Pleas within 30 days
- Appeal requires new filing fee
