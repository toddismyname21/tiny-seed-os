## 2. EMPLOYMENT BASICS

### Employment At-Will
Employment at Tiny Seed Farm is "at-will." This means either you or the farm may end the employment relationship at any time, for any reason, with or without notice or cause.

### Equal Employment Opportunity
Tiny Seed Farm provides equal employment opportunity to all employees and applicants regardless of race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, veteran status, sexual orientation, gender identity, or any other protected characteristic.

### Employment Categories

| Category | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Full-Time Year-Round** | 40+ hours/week, 12 months |
| **Full-Time Seasonal** | 40+ hours/week, seasonal (typically March-November) |
| **Part-Time** | Less than 40 hours/week |
| **H2A Workers** | Temporary agricultural workers under H2A visa program |

New employees serve a 30-day introductory period. This allows both you and the farm to evaluate fit. During this period, either party may end employment with minimal notice.

## 3. WORK HOURS & SCHEDULING

### Standard Farm Hours
The farm operates **6:00 AM - 7:00 PM** during the growing season, with extended hours during harvest.

### Work Week
- Standard work week: **Monday - Saturday**
- Sunday work: As needed during peak season, with advance notice
- Typical shift: 8-10 hours during growing season

### Scheduling
- Weekly schedules posted by **Thursday** for the following week
- Schedule changes require 24-hour notice when possible
- Swap shifts only with manager approval

### Overtime
- Non-exempt employees receive overtime pay (1.5x regular rate) for hours over 40 per week
- **Note:** Agricultural workers may be exempt from overtime under PA law. Your offer letter specifies your status.
- All overtime must be pre-approved by a manager

### Breaks
| Shift Length | Breaks |
|--------------|--------|
| 4-6 hours | One 15-minute break |
| 6-8 hours | One 30-minute meal break + one 15-minute break |
| 8+ hours | One 30-minute meal break + two 15-minute breaks |

Breaks should be taken as scheduled. Do not skip breaks - farm work is physically demanding and breaks are necessary for safety.

### Time Tracking
- Clock in when you arrive, clock out when you leave
- Record all time worked accurately
- Report any errors immediately
- Falsifying time records is grounds for termination

## 7. SAFETY & HEALTH

### Safety is Everyone's Responsibility
Farm work involves physical labor, equipment, and environmental hazards. Your safety is our priority, but you must actively participate.

### General Safety Rules
1. **Report hazards immediately** - don't assume someone else will
2. **Use equipment only after training**
3. **Wear appropriate PPE** (gloves, sunscreen, eye protection as needed)
4. **Stay hydrated** - especially in summer heat
5. **Lift properly** - bend at the knees, not the back
6. **Know your limits** - ask for help with heavy loads
7. **Keep work areas clean** - trip hazards are preventable

### Heat Safety
Pennsylvania summers are hot and humid. Heat illness is serious.

**Prevention:**
- Drink water frequently (at least 8oz every 20 minutes in heat)
- Take breaks in shade
- Wear light, breathable clothing
- Know the signs of heat exhaustion

**Signs of Heat Illness:**
- Heavy sweating or no sweating
- Weakness, dizziness
- Nausea
- Rapid heartbeat
- Confusion

**If you or a coworker shows signs:** Stop work immediately, move to shade, hydrate, and notify a manager. Call 911 if severe.

### Equipment Safety
- Never operate equipment without training
- Perform pre-use checks
- Report malfunctions immediately
- Keep guards and shields in place
- Turn off equipment before clearing jams
- No passengers on equipment designed for one operator

### Chemical Safety
As an organic farm, we use minimal chemicals. However:
- Read labels before using any product
- Wear required PPE
- Store chemicals properly
- Report spills immediately
- Never mix products unless instructed

### Injury Reporting
**Report ALL injuries immediately, no matter how minor.**

1. Stop work and assess the injury
2. Notify your manager
3. Complete an incident report
4. Seek medical attention if needed

Workers' compensation covers work-related injuries. Failure to report injuries may affect your coverage.

### First Aid
First aid kits are located:
- In the barn
- In each farm vehicle
- In the packing shed

Know where they are. If supplies are low, report it.

### Emergency Procedures
- **Fire:** Alert others, evacuate, call 911, meet at [designated area]
- **Severe Weather:** Move to barn, stay away from windows and equipment
- **Medical Emergency:** Call 911, administer first aid if trained, notify manager

## 8. FARM OPERATIONS

### Organic Certification Requirements
Our organic certification is essential. Violations can result in loss of certification, which would devastate our business.

**You MUST:**
- Use only approved inputs (seeds, fertilizers, pest control)
- Never bring prohibited substances onto organic fields
- Maintain separation from any non-organic materials
- Document everything (plantings, applications, harvests)
- Report any contamination concerns immediately

**Prohibited on Organic Land:**
- Synthetic fertilizers
- Synthetic pesticides/herbicides
- GMO seeds
- Non-approved substances

**When in doubt, ASK before using any product.**

### Food Safety
We grow food that people eat. Food safety is non-negotiable.

**Personal Hygiene:**
- Wash hands before handling produce
- Wash hands after using restroom, eating, smoking, or handling non-food items
- Cover cuts and wounds
- Do not work if you have vomiting, diarrhea, or fever

**Harvest Handling:**
- Keep produce off the ground
- Use clean containers
- Maintain cold chain for perishables
- Never harvest damaged or contaminated produce

### Documentation
Accurate records are required for:
- Organic certification
- Food safety traceability
- Business operations

When asked to record something (plantings, harvest weights, applications), do it completely and accurately.
